About the Programme

Coaching bright minds
from rural backgrounds

Students coming from disadvantaged backgrounds continue to have limited representation in national-level competitive examinations such as CUET, CLAT, NDA, JEE, and NEET. Such exams are important pathways to quality higher education, scholarships, and long-term career opportunities. However, many capable students lack the awareness, guidance, and academic support needed to prepare effectively for these opportunities.

The challenges often begin in high school, where students may not receive sufficient exposure to conceptual learning, analytical thinking, and problem-solving skills. To address this gap, we provide academically bright high school students with training in Mathematics and Science, with a focus on conceptual understanding, along with guidance and mentorship. Over the past five years, the programme has supported more than 200 students, many of whom are now studying in prestigious institutions, secured scholarships, and are looking forward to rewarding careers.

Programme Details
  • The programme is free of cost to students and their families
  • Classes are conducted for students in 9th and 10th standards
  • Classes are conducted online so that students in small towns and villages can benefit
  • Classes are conducted in the evening after regular school hours
  • We follow NCERT syllabus to prepare for national-level competition
  • Professionals from different fields teach students to build strong conceptual understanding in Science and Maths
  • Activities are conducted to make students aware of opportunities and preparation for higher education, scholarships, and entrance exams
Eligibility Criteria
  • You must have written the NMMS exam and scored 100 or more marks in total
  • Have at least 2 hours every day to dedicate to our classes
  • You can comfortably solve questions from textbooks and want to attempt more difficult questions
  • You want to develop a deeper understanding of concepts and build a strong base in Science and Maths
  • You want to attempt competitive exams such as scholarships, talent search, and entrance to colleges
How to Apply

For NMMS Class 8 coaching: attempt the sample question paper. If you answer at least 15 questions correctly, send your application on WhatsApp to 86603 32586. For Class 9 NTSE coaching: download the application form and send to pramodkulkarni4@gmail.com

Success Stories

Where our students
are today

Ningamma
Chikkabidarakallu Govt. School, Bangalore, Karnataka
Cohort 2020 · First Student

Ningamma's parents are farmers from North Karnataka who migrated to Bangalore. Her father drives a goods van and her mother works in a garments factory. Ningamma took our classes seriously and went on to score 619 out of 625 (99.33%) in her Class 10 board exam, securing 6th rank in Karnataka. She won free admission at Deeksha Integrated PU College. In March 2024, she secured 97% in her PU exam, appeared for JEE Mains, and secured admission at NITK, Surathkal, where she is now pursuing B.Tech. in Electronics and Communication.

Mahadev
Mudagunda Village, Chamarajanagar, Karnataka
IIT Kharagpur

Mahadev's father works in a ceramics factory earning around ₹7,000 per month. He studied at Adarsha Vidyalaya in Kollegal and scored 98% in Class 10. He won 1st prize at state-level quiz competitions, passed all 3 stages of the Rashtrotthana Parishat exam, and was selected for their Tapas programme. He received JEE coaching there and cleared JEE with a good ranking. He is now studying BSc in Physics at IIT Kharagpur and aspires to become an academic professor.

Chandrika
Jagadal Village, Bagalkot, Karnataka
BMSIT Bangalore · Two Scholarships

Chandrika is from a farming family of 4 children and studied in Kannada medium through Class 10, scoring 98.7%. She received free admission at Avanti Fellows, Hubli (typically costing ₹1 lakh per year) for 11th and 12th. Despite the challenge of adjusting to English medium, the in-depth training from our programme helped her succeed. Chandrika secured admission at BMSIT Bangalore in Computer Science and Engineering and also secured two scholarships, pursuing her education without financial burden on her parents.

Monisha
Bannur, T. Narasipura, Mysore, Karnataka
MBBS · Hassan Institute of Medical Sciences

Monisha travelled 13 km each way to school by bus but took academics very seriously, scoring 98% in her Class 10 boards. She passed the SAADHANA exam by Rashtrotthana Parishat and received free admission (including hostel) at SAADHANA, Bengaluru, for NEET coaching. Monisha secured a NEET rank of 18,000 (97th percentile) and is currently pursuing MBBS at Hassan Institute of Medical Sciences, a government medical college.

Jyothi
Koila Taluk, Dakshina Kannada, Karnataka
SADHANA Programme · NEET Aspirant

Jyothi's family moved from Gulbarga to Dakshina Kannada in search of work. Both parents are labourers. She joined our online classes in 2023-24 and, though she initially faced difficulty with technical English from a Kannada-medium background, grew to participate confidently in class discussions. She passed all 3 stages of the Rashtrotthana Parishat exam and secured a place in the SADHANA programme, which offers free education and hostel for 11th and 12th. Jyothi is now in 11th standard with her eyes set on cracking NEET to become a doctor.
