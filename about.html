Our Story

From one platform
to all of India

SATHI — Society for Assistance to Children in Difficult Situations — was founded in Pune in 1992 by a small group of social workers who kept seeing the same thing: children, alone, on railway platforms. Nobody's job to help. Nobody doing anything.

They decided it would be their job. Thirty-two years later, SATHI operates across seven cities, runs four distinct programmes, and has reached over 30,000 children. The work is the same as it was in 1992 — find the child, earn their trust, bring them home.

We are a registered Indian non-profit with FCRA certification, governed by a board of trustees and supported by corporates, individual donors, and government partnerships. Our team of over 80 full-time staff includes outreach workers, counsellors, social workers, and educators — most of whom have been with us for a decade or more.

Our Founder
The man who
started it all.
"Every child, no matter how far they have wandered, deserves to be found, heard, and brought safely home."

SATHI was founded in 1992 by Pramod Kulkarni, a man who had encountered children living on Mumbai's Victoria Terminus and could not look away. What he saw there stayed with him: children who had fallen into cycles of survival built around platforms, addictions, and the slow erosion of any idea of home.

He registered SATHI formally in 1997 and spent the rest of his life building it into a national force for child protection, rigorous in its systems, patient in its relationships, and uncompromising in its belief that every child belongs somewhere safe.

Pramod Kulkarni is no longer with us. His work continues in every child SATHI brings home.

Our Journey

1992
Founded in Pune

SATHI begins outreach at Pune Railway Station, the first organised effort to identify and assist children stranded on railway platforms in the city.

1998
First shelter home opens

SATHI opens its first dedicated shelter home in Pune, giving rescued children a safe, child-friendly space for the first time.

2004
Expansion to Bangalore & Delhi

SATHI extends its railway outreach programme to Bangalore and Delhi — two of India's largest railway hubs — significantly increasing its reach.

2009
Nurture Merit launched

Recognising that rescue without long-term support is incomplete, SATHI launches its education scholarship programme for children from the most disadvantaged backgrounds.

2015
Migrant & Slum programmes begin

SATHI expands beyond the railway platform to reach children of migrant workers and slum communities in Pune — children who are equally invisible to the system.

2024
30,000 children reached

SATHI crosses 30,000 children rescued and assisted across all programmes — a milestone that reflects three decades of quiet, persistent work.

What We Believe

01
Child First, Always

Every decision we make — every protocol, every intervention — centres the child's dignity, safety, and long-term wellbeing above all else.

02
No Judgement

We meet every child — and every family — where they are. We do not ask how they got there. We ask how we can help them move forward.

03
Trust Takes Time

A child who has been hurt does not trust easily. Our workers are trained to be patient — to sit, to listen, to wait as long as it takes.

04
Community Rooted

Our outreach workers come from the same communities as the children they serve. That proximity is not incidental — it is the foundation of what we do.

05
Systemic Change

We work with government systems, not around them — building institutional capacity and advocating for policy change alongside our direct work.

06
Accountable Always

We publish annual reports, undergo independent audits, and hold ourselves to the highest standards of financial and programmatic transparency.
