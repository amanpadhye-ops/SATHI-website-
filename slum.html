The Problem

The city that
doesn't see them

Pune is one of India's fastest-growing cities. But the informal settlements on its edges are home to hundreds of thousands of families living without basic amenities — and without their children having access to quality education or child protection systems.

Children in these communities face overcrowded schools, unsafe home environments, early marriage, child labour, and a complete absence of the kind of safe, stimulating spaces that children in other parts of the city take for granted. SATHI's community centres fill that gap.

What Our Centres Do

01
Safe Spaces

Children have a place to be children — to play, learn, and grow in safety, away from the pressures and dangers of life in cramped settlements.

02
Education Support

Homework help, bridge classes, and remedial education ensure children keep pace with school — and have somewhere to go when school lets them down.

03
Child Rights Awareness

Children learn about their rights — the right to education, to safety, to be heard. Communities are engaged to understand and uphold those rights.

04
Family Engagement

We work with parents and families — supporting mothers through women's groups, and engaging fathers in understanding the value of their children's education.

From the Community

Priya, 12
Yerwada, Pune

Priya's parents were domestic workers who left home before dawn every day. She would sit in a one-room house with her younger siblings until it was time for school — and often, she simply didn't go. SATHI's community centre became her anchor. She enrolled in the morning programme, brought her siblings along, and within a year had not missed a single day of school. She is now part of the centre's child leadership group, helping other children in her neighbourhood.

Arjun, 10
Kasba Peth, Pune

Arjun's school had flagged him as a "dropout risk" — he was two years behind his grade level and had missed more days than he'd attended in the previous year. The SATHI centre in his neighbourhood offered him daily tutoring, and within six months his grades had improved enough that his school reinstated him at his correct grade level. His teacher said she had never seen a turnaround so fast.
